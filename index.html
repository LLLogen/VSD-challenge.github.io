Abstract

We propose the visual spatial description (Visual Spatial Description) challenge on the platform of ACM MM. This challenge falls within the research domain of visual spatial semantics understanding. In the Visual Spatial Description challenge, models and systems are expected to generate an accurate textual descriptive sentence of the spatial relationship between two given target visual objects in an input image. Alongside the challenge, a large-scale Visual Spatial Description dataset will be provided, consisting of 29,272 high-quality manually annotated image-text pairs.

There are real images derived from Visual Genome and SpatialSense dataset, containing a variety of entities. The annotators are required to extract the spatial relationship of the given two objects, and then generate a sentence to describe their spatial relation open-ended.

Challenge Task

Task-1: Classification of Visual Spatial Relationship. Participants are required to construct models that can extract spatial relationships between two given objects O₁ and O₂ and output a triplet containing the spatial relationships of them. The relationships could be chosen from nine labels: “on”, “in”, “next”, “under”, “above”, “behind”, “in front of”, “left”, and “right”. The evaluation of this sub-task is the F1 score of the multi-label classification.

Task-2: Description of Single Spatial Relationship. Participants are required to build models that generates a textual description of the single spatial relationship between the two objects O₁ and O₂. There are one or more ground-truth for each data entry. We calculate BLEU-4 and SPICE for the predicted sentence and each ground truth and choose the max score. We rank the submitted models by a weighted sum z of BLEU-4 and SPICE score: z = 0.4BLEU4 + 0.6SPICE

Task-3: Description of open-ended spatial relationship. In this task, we provide a more challenging dataset that contains more complex spatial description. Contestants are required to construct models that can generate textual description to describe the spatial relationship between O₁ and O₂. Different to task-2, in the task-3, the model should generate 3 diverse descriptions. The evaluation of task-3 contains two parts, the Correctness and the Diversity. For the correctness we use the same metrics as task-2. For the diversity, we use mBLEU-4 and SPICE5.
